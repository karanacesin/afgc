What We Believe (A Bible Study)

The Bible is the inspired Word of God. (Please see - 2 Timothy 3:16-17; Galatians 1:12; 2 Peter 1:19-21; 1 Corinthians 14:37; Luke 4:3-5)

There is One God, maker of all things, being in Trinity of Father, Son and Holy Spirit. (Please see - Deuteronomy 6:4; 1 Corinthians 8:4; Galatians 3:20; 1 Timothy 2:5; Matthew 3:16-17, Matthew 28:19; 2 Corinthians 13:14)

Jesus Christ, the incarnate Son of God was begotten by the Holy Spirit, born of a virgin and is true God and man. (Jesus is God: John 1:1; John 8:48-59; John 5:18. Jesus is the Son of God: Daniel 3:25; Matthew 14:32-34; Matthew 27:53-55. Virgin birth: Gen.3:15; Isa.7:14; Matthew 1:22-24

There is personal slavation for believers through the shed Blood fo Jesus Christ. (Please see - 1 Peter 1:10-12; John 3:16-18; Acts 2:20-22;

Water Baptism following conversion is not a requirement of salvation but is an act of obedience of the believer.

The Baptism of the Holy Spirit, accompanied by the initial sign of speeking in other tongues, is distinct from the new birth experience.

The gifts of the Holy Spirit listed in 1 Corinthians 12 are all available to all believers.

There is divine healing, through faith, included in the Atonement.

The casting out of demons is an essential part of Christ's commistion to the Church.

Intensive world evangelism and missionary work, with signs following, is the Lord's Great Commission to the Church.

There will be a resurrection of the dead with eternal happiness for the saved, eternal punishment for the lost.

The Christian's hope is the imminenet, personal return of the Lord Jesus Christ.

The Lord's Supper is to be remembered by the Church.
